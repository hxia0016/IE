Why reduce food waste is important?

Victorian households dispose an average of 250,000 tonnes worth of edible food to landfills. Food decomposition generates greenhouse gases that is harmful for public health and air quality.

Avoid disposing food scraps into recycling bin: Food waste is also identified as one of the highest recycling bin contaminants and should not be place in recycling bin.

Reduce food waste

Planning

Check what you have and list down the things you need only. You can try to plan your meals two days ahead or weekly. Don’t forget to include the probability of leftovers as well.

Donate

Donate edible food to food distribution charity such as Foodbank, Secondbite, or OzHarvest.


Cooking

  • Cook according to your meal plan

  • Cook according to your meal plan

  • Use ingredients creatively

  • Have it collected from your home through eMeals





Pantry

  • Understand the dates: Best Before means that the food will start to deteriorate after the date but is still edible.

  • Airtight storage: Half-used food packets should be stored in sealed containers to keep the freshness.

  • First in, first out: Placed newer items at the back and older item Infront.

Fridge

  • Keep the temperature cool: The suggested temperature should be around 3 to 4 degrees Celsius

  • Avoid overloading: Air circulation is important for temperature stability

  • Separate cooked and raw foods: Sealed raw meat and kept on bottom shelf to avoid leakage.

  • Have it collected from your home through eMeals
